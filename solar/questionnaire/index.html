inverter mode

An inverter is a critical part of a solar system — it converts the electricity your solar panels make and turns it into the kind your home can use.


3. Which inverter mode is most applicable?

Your solar system will stay connected to the national grid, meaning you can use grid power when needed and send excess solar power back for credit. Your installation will operate independently, where your home relies solely on solar panels and batteries for power.

black-out prevention

Standard grid-tied solar inverters automatically shut down during a grid outage — meaning your home will lose power even if the sun is shining.

At Burmark, we recommend installing solar systems with blackout protection. These systems automatically isolate from the grid during an outage and continue supplying power to your installation, ensuring greater resilience and energy security.


4. Would you like black-out prevention?

All appliances and circuits will work during a grid blackout. Selected circuits & appliances will function during a grid blackout. You will be without power if during a grid blackout.

energy storage systems

Batteries store excess energy produced during the day so you can use it at night or when the sun isn’t shining.

Batteries help reduce reliance on the grid during expensive peak hours and provide backup power during blackouts, making your home more energy independent.
