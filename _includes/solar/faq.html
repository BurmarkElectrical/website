If your system includes blackout protection / changeover capability, then you will continue to have power during a grid outage.

Standard grid-connected solar systems automatically shut down when the grid goes down. This safety feature (anti-islanding protection) prevents electricity from feeding back into the network while repairs are being carried out.

Without changeover functionality your solar will switch off during a blackout.

It depends on your goals, budget and power usage.

As battery technology becomes more affordable and intelligent, we can prioritise self-consumption — meaning you use as much of your own solar energy as possible instead of exporting it to the grid. With smart charge and discharge scheduling, we can control when the battery supplies your home and when it’s better to import from the grid (for example, avoiding peak pricing).

If your home already uses most of its energy during daylight hours, solar-only systems often provide the fastest financial return, just remember solar is a long term investment and a battery can save you more in the long term.

As standard, we evaluate both solar-only and solar with battery configurations, so we can recommend the option that best suits your circumstances and budget.

Excess solar energy you produce can be exported to the grid and bought back later, but it's usually a case of selling low and buying high.
